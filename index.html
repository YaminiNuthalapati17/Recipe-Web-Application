Butter Chicken Biryani

Ingredients

  • **Chicken:**
    • 750 grams Tandoori chicken, cut into pieces on bone and half-cooked
  • **Rice:**
    • 3 cups Cooked rice
  • **For the Gravy:**
    • 2-3 tablespoons Oil
    • ¼ cup Butter
    • 2-3 Green chillies, slit
    • 1 inch Ginger, cut into strips
    • 1 Mace blade
    • 3-4 Green cardamoms
    • 3-4 medium Onions, chopped
    • 1 tablespoon Ginger-garlic paste
    • 3 cups Tomato puree
    • 1½ teaspoons Garam masala powder
    • ½ teaspoon Dried fenugreek leaves (kasoori methi)
    • Salt to taste
  • **For Garnishing (Optional):**
    • 2 tablespoons Fresh cream
    • 1 tablespoon Honey
    • 2 tablespoons Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
    • 8-9 Fresh mint leaves
    • 1 cup Browned Onions

Instructions

    1. In a large pan, heat oil and butter over medium heat. Add green chilies, ginger, mace blade, and cardamoms. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
    2. Add chopped onions and cook until softened and translucent, about 5-7 minutes.
    3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for another minute, stirring constantly.
    4. Pour in the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates from the masala paste, around 5-7 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent burning.
    5. Add garam masala powder, kasoori methi, and salt to taste. Mix well and cook for another minute.
    6. Add the cooked tandoori chicken pieces and gently combine with the masala.
    7. In a separate pot, layer half of the cooked rice followed by the prepared chicken gravy. Repeat with the remaining rice.
    8. **Optional Ghee Method:** Drizzle some ghee over the top layer of rice. (Ghee adds a richer flavor but is not mandatory)
    9. Cover the pot tightly with a lid and seal the edges with dough made from wheat flour and water (optional but helps trap steam for even cooking). Alternatively, wrap the lid tightly with aluminum foil.
    10. Cook on low heat for about 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is heated through.
    11. **Optional Garnishing:** While the biryani cooks, prepare the garnishing (if using). In a small pan, heat some cream and honey until slightly warmed. Stir in chopped coriander leaves and mint leaves.
    12. Turn off the heat and let the biryani rest for another 5-10 minutes before serving.
    13. Gently fluff the rice with a fork and spoon the biryani onto a serving dish. Drizzle with the prepared cream mixture (if using) and garnish with browned onions and fresh coriander sprigs.
    14. Enjoy your delicious butter chicken biryani!

Tomato Dosa

Ingredients

  • **For the Dosa Batter:**
    • 2 cups Urad Dal (Whole Black Gram), rinsed and soaked for 4-6 hours
    • 3 cups Parboiled Rice, rinsed and soaked for 4-6 hours
    • 1 tsp Methi Dana (Fenugreek Seeds), rinsed and soaked for 30 minutes (optional)
    • Salt to taste
  • **For the Tomato Filling:**
    • 2 tbsp Cooking Oil
    • 1 tsp Mustard Seeds
    • 1-2 Green Chillies, slit (adjust for spice preference)
    • 2-3 Curry Leaves
    • 1 medium Onion, finely chopped
    • 2 large Tomatoes, finely chopped
    • ½ tsp Turmeric Powder
    • ½ tsp Red Chili Powder (adjust for spice preference)
    • ½ tsp Coriander Powder
    • Salt to taste
    • Chopped fresh Coriander leaves, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. **Preparing the Dosa Batter:**
    • In a large bowl, combine the rinsed and soaked urad dal, parboiled rice, and fenugreek seeds (if using). Grind the mixture into a smooth batter using a blender or grinder. Add water as needed to achieve a pouring consistency (slightly thicker than pancake batter).
    • Add salt to taste and mix well. Cover the batter and let it ferment in a warm place for at least 8 hours, or until doubled in volume.
  2. **Making the Tomato Filling:**
    • Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter.
    • Add green chilies, curry leaves, and chopped onions. Sauté until the onions become translucent.
    • Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften and become mushy. Mash the tomatoes with a spatula if needed.
    • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt to taste. Mix well and cook for a minute, allowing the spices to release their aroma.
    • Turn off the heat and garnish with chopped coriander leaves (optional).
  3. **Cooking the Dosa:**
    • Heat a flat griddle or tawa over medium heat. Once hot, grease it lightly with oil.
    • Pour a ladleful of batter onto the griddle and spread it into a thin circle using the back of the ladle. Drizzle a few drops of oil around the edges of the dosa.
    • Cook for a minute or two, until the bottom becomes slightly golden brown and the dosa loosens from the griddle.
    • Spread some prepared tomato filling onto the cooked dosa surface.
    • Fold the dosa in half, then fold it again into a triangle or desired shape.
    • Serve the tomato dosa hot with coconut chutney or sambar.

Masala Peanuts

Ingredients

  • 1 cup raw peanuts
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 pinch asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 dry red chilies, broken into pieces
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust for spice preference)
  • ½ teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Chopped curry leaves (optional)
  • Chopped fresh coriander leaves, for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add asafoetida and cumin seeds. Let the cumin seeds splutter for a few seconds.
  2. Add dry red chilies and fry for a few seconds until fragrant.
  3. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and coriander powder. Mix well and cook for about 30 seconds.
  4. Add the raw peanuts and stir to coat them with the spices.
  5. Reduce heat to low and roast the peanuts for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they become crisp and golden brown.
  6. Add salt to taste and mix well.
  7. Turn off the heat and garnish with chopped curry leaves (if using) and fresh coriander leaves (if using).
  8. Let the masala peanuts cool slightly before serving. Enjoy as a delicious and protein-rich snack!

Green Tea

Ingredients

  • 1 green tea bag (or 1-2 teaspoons of loose leaf green tea)
  • 230ml (8oz) hot water (ideally between 70-80°C / 158-176°F)
  • (Optional) Honey, lemon, or other desired sweetener

Instructions

  1. Heat water to the desired temperature (ideally between 70-80°C / 158-176°F). Using water that is too hot can burn the tea leaves and result in a bitter taste.
  2. Place the green tea bag (or loose leaf tea) in your cup or teapot.
  3. Pour the hot water over the tea leaves.
  4. (Optional) Steep the tea for 1-3 minutes, depending on your desired strength. Generally, steeping for a longer time will result in a stronger tea.
  5. Remove the tea bag or strain the loose leaf tea.
  6. (Optional) Add honey, lemon, or other desired sweetener to taste.
  7. Enjoy your cup of green tea!
